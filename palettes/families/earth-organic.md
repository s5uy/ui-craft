# Palette Family: Earth Organic

## Character

Natural, grounded tones drawn from stone, clay, soil, olive, and natural fiber. Communicates: authenticity, sustainability, craft, and physical groundedness. Where neutral-warm is a subtle warmth, earth-organic is deliberate about its reference to the natural world. Use when the product's identity is meaningfully connected to nature, health, food, wellness, or artisan craft.

## Variation Axes

**More terracotta (clay-led):** Background `#F2EBE0`, surface `#E8DFCF`, border `#CEC0AA`. Richer, more handcrafted feeling. Use for artisan, ceramics, or food contexts.

**More olive/forest (nature-forward):** Background `#F0F2EC`, surface `#E6E8E0`, text `#1A1E16`. Shifts the warmth toward green. Use for wellness, sustainability, botanical products.

**Lighter stone (minimal earth):** Background `#FAFAF7`, surface `#F5F2EC`. Very light earth reference — barely perceptible but distinctly non-pure-white. Use for minimal products that want warmth without conspicuous earthiness.

**Deep soil (rich dark):** Background `#140F08`, surface `#1E170E`. Maximum earthiness in dark mode. Use for premium food/beverage brands.

## Accent Compatibility

Earth-organic foundations need accents that feel native to the natural palette — not digital or synthetic.

| Accent | Hex | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Terracotta | `#C2643A` | Clay, craft, ceramics, Mediterranean |
| Forest green | `#2D6A4F` or `#166534` | Botanical, organic, sustainability |
| Ochre | `#B5860D` | Harvest, warmth, artisan |
| Burnt sienna | `#A0522D` | Earthy, rich, handmade |
| Olive | `#6B7C3A` | Botanical, fresh, outdoor |
| Warm navy | `#1E3A5F` | Grounding, traditional craft |

**Avoid:**
- Electric blue, cyan, neon — synthetic, anti-organic
- Purple — rarely fits natural product contexts
- Bright saturated colors — break the natural register
- Cool gray accents — temperature conflict

## Typography Interaction

Earth-organic palettes pair exceptionally well with typefaces that carry warmth and craftsmanship:

**Best typefaces:**
- Humanist serifs (Freight Text, Sentinel, Lora) — warmth and authority
- Humanist sans (Gill Sans, Aktiv Grotesk) — organic but legible
- Hand-influenced geometric (Söhne, Circular — restrained) — modern but not cold
- Avoid: geometric cold sans (Neue Haas), ultra-thin display weights, industrial grotesque

**Weight:** Medium-to-heavy for headings feels grounded. Very thin weights feel discordant against the rich earthiness of the palette.

**Contrast:** The warm dark-brown text against sandy backgrounds has slightly lower intrinsic contrast than black-on-white. Ensure body text (`#1E1A14` on `#F5F2EB`) meets WCAG AA contrast ratios. Test before shipping.

---

## Density Guidance

Earth-organic palettes support low-to-medium density well. High density is possible but loses the organic quality.

- **Product pages, landing pages:** Excellent — generous spacing and warm surfaces create a premium, natural feel.
- **Blog / editorial:** Good — pairs naturally with neutral-warm content sections.
- **Dense admin UI:** Not recommended — the earthy tones are too textural for efficient scanning.
- **Mobile-first:** Works well — the warmth translates effectively to smaller screens.

## When to Use

- Consumer health, wellness, and fitness products
- Food, beverage, and hospitality brands
- Sustainability and environmental tech products
- Artisan, craft, and maker-oriented products
- Agriculture and farming technology
- Natural beauty and skincare brands
- Outdoor, adventure, and nature-connected products

---

## When NOT to Use

- Products that need to signal cutting-edge technology or digital precision
- Financial or compliance products (earthiness undermines trust signals)
- Developer tools or technical infrastructure
- Products with a primarily digital/screen-native identity
- Any context where users expect a "modern tech" aesthetic
