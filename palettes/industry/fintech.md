# Industry Palette: Fintech & Banking

## Industry Context

Fintech deals with money, which inherently means high stakes and the need for absolute trust. The fundamental user need is clarity, tracking, and reassurance that their capital is secure. The interface must communicate stability, precision, and institutional reliability without feeling outdated.

---

## Default AI Archetype (What to Avoid)

AI defaults to putting a bright neon "crypto green" on every button, chart, and active state, often accompanied by a dark blue background. 
- **The Cliché:** Bright neon green (`#00FF00` or similar variants) on a deep navy or black background.
- **Why it fails:** It looks like a volatile retail day-trading app or a Web3 scam, not a reliable place to store serious capital. It induces anxiety rather than calm.

---

## Recommended Palette Families

### 1. High Trust
**Why it works:** The muted blues and conservative neutrals of the High Trust family signal institutional weight. It tells the user "we are responsible with your money." It is the safest and most professional choice for wealth management, banking, and B2B finance.

### 2. Neutral Cool
**Why it works:** The blue-gray undertones provide an efficient, modern feel. Perfect for modern ledger apps, expense tracking, and modern banking platforms that want to feel digital-native but still completely serious.

### 3. Deep Technical
**Why it works:** For professional trading terminals, analytics, and complex treasury management. The near-black surfaces allow complex, multi-variable financial data to be parsed quickly over long sessions.

---

## Accent Strategy

- **Single Accent Discipline:** Crucial. Pick one extremely restrained brand color (often a deep blue, muted teal, or dark forest green).
- **Functional Supremacy:** In fintech, Green (profit/success) and Red (loss/error) carry intense specific meaning. The decorative accent *must never* be green or red unless the brand exclusively uses that color and clearly distinguishes it from data indicators.
- **Color as Data:** Use color strictly to convey moving numbers, not to decorate the container.

---

## Typography Integration

- **Scale:** Needs to accommodate dense tables of numbers without feeling cramped. Use fixed-width (tabular) numerals for all financial figures.
- **Contrast:** High legibility is required. A user must not misread a digit. Use strong hierarchy between the primary balance and the secondary metadata.

---

## Anti-Patterns

- 🚫 **Using Green as the Primary Brand Color:** It completely breaks the ability to use green for "profit" or "positive change."
- 🚫 **Gradients on Financial Cards:** Adds visual noise to areas where the user wants hard facts. Shows a lack of maturity.
- 🚫 **Playful or "Fun" Accents:** Yellow, bright orange, or hot pink should be avoided unless explicitly requested for a disruptive Gen-Z finance brand. Money is serious.
