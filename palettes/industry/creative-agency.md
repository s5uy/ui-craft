# Industry Palette: Creative Agency & Portfolio

## Industry Context

Agency sites, design studios, and freelance portfolios exist solely to showcase the creator's work and taste. The product is not the website; the product is the work *displayed* on the website. The user need is inspiration, capability validation, and contact information.

---

## Default AI Archetype (What to Avoid)

AI usually generates "bento box" grids with massive gradients, or attempts a "playful" startup look with quirky illustrations.
- **The Cliché:** A dark purple gradient background with glassmorphism cards and a centered "We build digital experiences" hero text.
- **Why it fails:** It looks exactly like every other AI-generated site. It screams "I used a template" and completely overrides whatever actual portfolio work is being shown.

---

## Recommended Palette Families

### 1. Monochrome Systems
**Why it works:** The gallery wall principle. A museum paints its walls white or gray so the art speaks. A portfolio should be monochrome so the project imagery carries the color.

### 2. Paper Editorial
**Why it works:** Provides a high-end, classic print design aesthetic. Exceptional for typography-led portfolios (like a copywriter or a brand strategist) where words are the primary asset.

### 3. Editorial Brutalism
**Why it works:** Harsh, extremely high contrast (often pure `#000000` text on `#EEEEEE` or vice-versa). Distinct borders, exposed structure. It signals a sophisticated, contemporary design point of view.

---

## Accent Strategy

- **Zero Accent (Recommended):** Use no accent color. All links are underlined, hovers invert the background.
- **The "Punch" Accent:** One single, extremely aggressive color (e.g., Neon Chartreuse `#DFFF00` or International Klein Blue `#002FA7`) used *only* for the contact button or a custom cursor.

---

## Typography Integration

- **Scale:** Extreme. Massive, screen-filling typography (`text-7xl` or larger) contrasted immediately with very small, tightly kerned tracking details.
- **Type Families:** This is the place to use custom, expressive fonts. Brutalist sans-serifs (like Helvetica Now, Neue Haas Grotesk) or highly stylized display serifs.

---

## Anti-Patterns

- 🚫 **Colored Backgrounds:** Immediately clashes with project photography or video reels.
- 🚫 **Soft Shadows:** Looks cheap in a high-design context. Use hard shadows (`shadow-[4px_4px_0_0_#000]`) or no shadows at all with borders.
- 🚫 **Generic System Fonts:** Using Inter or Roboto in an agency portfolio instantly robs it of distinction. Type is the main design tool here.
