# Blueprint Selection Guide

Use this to choose the right blueprint — or blend — for a given product. Work through the three filters in sequence.

---

## Filter 1: Primary Product Category

| Product category | Primary blueprint | Consider blending |
|---|---|---|
| Dashboard / analytics / monitoring | Command Center | — |
| Infrastructure / DevOps / CI-CD | Command Center | — |
| Trading / financial terminal | Command Center | — |
| SaaS homepage / marketing | Spatial Immersive | Enterprise Neutral (if B2B) |
| Startup launch / product reveal | Spatial Immersive | — |
| Consumer tech / hardware product page | Spatial Immersive | Editorial Warm |
| Creative agency / studio | Editorial Brutalism | — |
| Fashion / culture / art institution | Editorial Brutalism | — |
| Independent media / publication | Editorial Brutalism | Editorial Warm |
| B2B SaaS product UI | Enterprise Neutral | — |
| Admin panel / management interface | Enterprise Neutral | — |
| Internal tools / operations | Enterprise Neutral | Command Center |
| Settings / billing / account | Enterprise Neutral | — |
| Documentation site | Editorial Warm | Enterprise Neutral (technical docs) |
| Blog / content platform | Editorial Warm | — |
| Personal portfolio (writing-first) | Editorial Warm | Editorial Brutalism |
| Portfolio (design/dev, visual-first) | Editorial Brutalism | Spatial Immersive |

---

## Filter 2: Audience Trust Requirements

How much does the audience need to trust this product before using it?

| Trust level | Implication |
|---|---|
| **Low** (entertainment, creative, exploration) | Any blueprint is appropriate |
| **Medium** (productivity tools, communication apps) | Avoid extreme Editorial Brutalism; Spatial Immersive or Enterprise Neutral |
| **High** (finance, health, legal, compliance) | Enterprise Neutral strongly preferred; no Brutalism; muted Spatial Immersive |
| **Very high** (banking, medical, government) | Enterprise Neutral only; conservative palette; no motion ambition |

---

## Filter 3: Emotional Register

What should the product feel like?

| Target feeling | Blueprint alignment |
|---|---|
| Technically authoritative, precise | Command Center |
| Premium, atmospheric, confident | Spatial Immersive |
| Graphic, bold, unapologetic | Editorial Brutalism |
| Reliable, efficient, professional | Enterprise Neutral |
| Warm, thoughtful, human | Editorial Warm |

---

## Blend Rules

Blending two blueprints is valid when the product genuinely sits at the intersection. Follow these rules:

**Name the primary and secondary.** "Spatial Immersive with Enterprise Neutral influence on the product sections" is a valid approach. "A blend of all five" is not.

**Apply blueprints to regions, not elements.** A SaaS homepage might use Spatial Immersive for the marketing hero and feature sections, then Enterprise Neutral for the product screenshots and dashboard preview. The blend has geographic logic on the page.

**Never blend emotional opposites.** Editorial Brutalism and Editorial Warm are opposing aesthetics. Command Center and Spatial Immersive have different density requirements. Forcing these together produces incoherence, not character.

### Common valid blends

**Spatial Immersive + Enterprise Neutral:**
Marketing shell in Spatial Immersive style; product UI screenshots and demo sections rendered in Enterprise Neutral aesthetic. Good for B2B SaaS homepages where the product is a dashboard.

**Command Center + Enterprise Neutral:**
High-density monitoring or trading interface that also needs to handle forms, settings, and configuration. Command Center for live data panels; Enterprise Neutral for admin and configuration surfaces.

**Editorial Brutalism + Editorial Warm:**
Media brand or cultural institution where the editorial content itself uses warm reading typography, but section headers, navigation, and structural elements use the graphic brutalist approach.

**Editorial Warm + Spatial Immersive:**
Documentation site for a premium product. Content reading experience uses Editorial Warm. The landing page and feature overview use restrained Spatial Immersive elements.

## When None Fit Cleanly

If no single blueprint or blend is obviously correct, ask:
1. What does the user's primary task require — reading, monitoring, creating, or deciding?
2. What is the buyer's primary trust signal — visual sophistication, professional reliability, or transparent simplicity?
3. What would feel wrong — too flashy? Too dense? Too cold? Too casual?

The answer to "what would feel wrong" is often the fastest path to the right blueprint. Eliminate first.
