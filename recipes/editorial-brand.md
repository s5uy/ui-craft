# Recipe: Editorial Brand

For agencies, design studios, media publications, cultural institutions, portfolio sites.

---

## Scope

**Use for:**
- Design / branding / creative agencies
- Studios (product, film, motion, audio)
- Online magazines or editorial sites
- Cultural orgs, galleries, festivals
- High-end personal portfolios

**Not for:**
- Products with a conversion goal (landing pages → use a product recipe)
- Dashboards / app shells

---

## Design System

- **Blueprint:** Editorial Brutalism
- **Palette:** high contrast — choose one:
  - **Paper black** — `#f4f0e9` bg with near-black text and single saturated accent
  - **Ink:** deep near-black `#0c0c0c` with warm off-white
  - **Signal:** pure white with a single electric accent (safety orange, acid green)
- **Typography:** a display serif (Canela, Tiempos, Noe) OR a bold grotesk (Neue Haas, Söhne) for headlines + plain Inter/GT America for body. Often only two faces total.
- **Motion:** confident, near-instant. 150–300ms. No scroll theatrics.
- **Signature patterns:** marquee, split-flap text, aggressive grid overlays, large editorial images with captions set in mono

---

## Section Order (loose — editorial allows more freedom)

| # | Section | Job |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Nav | Often minimal: wordmark + 3–5 links in all-caps or small-caps |
| 2 | Hero | Typographic. Could be a 200px headline split across 3 lines. Often no image. Sets the voice in one gesture. |
| 3 | WorkGrid OR FeaturedPiece | Either a grid of projects with oversized images + mono captions, OR one featured case study with a long editorial treatment |
| 4 | About/Manifesto | A long-form paragraph block — a few sentences that define the studio's point of view. Often the typographic high point of the site. |
| 5 | ServicesOrCapabilities | Numbered list (01, 02, 03) with bold short labels. Not cards. |
| 6 | Team or Clients | List of names (people or clients), often in a marquee or a dense grid |
| 7 | Contact | Oversized `hello@studio.com` + a few lines. Not a form. |
| 8 | Footer | Minimal: wordmark + location + year + legal. |

---

## Voice Cues

- Allowed: strong, direct, opinionated. "We make things that feel inevitable." / "Design for companies that have something to say."
- Forbidden: every enterprise word, every "solutions" word, every SaaS formulation
- Tone: confident to the edge of arrogant. Studios earn it with the work.

---

## Failure Signals

- ❌ A SaaS-shaped hero (headline + subhead + primary+secondary button centered)
- ❌ A 3-col icon feature grid
- ❌ Pricing (editorial brand sites never show pricing)
- ❌ A contact form (a giant email address is the convention)
- ❌ Generic stock photography in the work grid
